Perplexity in the Real World

Tested workflows that take advantage of Perplexity's specific strengths — real-time sourced information, academic search, progressive narrowing, and the combination of Perplexity research feeding into Claude or ChatGPT for deeper work. These are the workflows that make Perplexity indispensable.

Research workflows

Research
Field survey before going deep
What are the main schools of thought / approaches / competing technologies in [field]? Give me the landscape with the key players, core debates, and what's changed in the last year.
Before investing hours researching a topic, spend five minutes getting a sourced landscape. You'll know which threads to pull, which sources matter, and what questions to ask. This is the first query for any new research project.
Academic · Pro
Literature survey on any research question
[Switch to Academic focus mode] What does the research say about [specific question]? Summarize the key findings, note any significant disagreements between studies, and flag what remains understudied or contested. Follow-up: What are the three most cited papers on this topic and what did each find?
Academic focus restricts sources to peer-reviewed research. You get a synthesized literature survey with real citations to actual papers — in minutes, not hours. This workflow replaces an entire afternoon of Google Scholar browsing for most research questions.
Research
Fact-checking claims before you use them
I've read that [specific claim]. Is this accurate according to current sources? What's the actual data, and what's the most authoritative source for this?
Perplexity is the fastest fact-checker available. Give it a claim, get back sourced verification or correction. The cited sources let you trace the claim to its origin. Use this before repeating any statistic or assertion in something you're writing or presenting.

Professional and business workflows

Business
Competitive intelligence sweep
What has [competitor] announced, launched, or changed in the last 3 months? Include pricing changes, product updates, partnerships, hiring signals, and any significant press coverage. Follow-up: How have their customers responded to [specific change]? [Switch to Reddit focus for the follow-up]
The combination is the key: web search for official announcements, Reddit focus for real customer reactions. You get both the signal and the sentiment, sourced and current, in two queries.
Professional
Pre-meeting intelligence
I'm meeting with [person's name] from [company] tomorrow. What should I know about them, their company's recent activities, and the current state of [their industry]? Focus on the last 6 months.
Five minutes of Perplexity research before a meeting produces more relevant context than 30 minutes of LinkedIn browsing. You walk in knowing what's happened recently at their company, what their industry is dealing with, and what's likely on their mind.
Research → Claude
The Perplexity-to-Claude pipeline
Step 1 in Perplexity: "Give me a comprehensive overview of [topic] with current data and key sources." Step 2 — copy the Perplexity answer into Claude: "Here's research I gathered on [topic]. Based on this information, [analyze / write / argue / strategize about a specific question]."
This is the highest-leverage workflow in your AI toolkit. Perplexity provides current, sourced factual grounding. Claude provides depth of analysis, quality of writing, and honest reasoning. Neither alone is as powerful as the combination. Research in Perplexity, think and write in Claude.

Student and learning workflows

Learning
Understanding a complex topic from scratch
Explain [concept] as if I'm encountering it for the first time. Start with why it matters, then explain the core idea, then tell me the key debates or misconceptions around it. Follow-up: What's the most important thing most people get wrong about [concept]? Follow-up: What should I read first to understand this more deeply?
The progressive follow-ups produce a learning roadmap with sourced recommendations — more reliable than asking an LLM for book recommendations that may be outdated or hallucinated.
The workflow pattern across all of these

Notice what every Perplexity workflow has in common: a specific question with time anchoring, progressive follow-ups that drill down, and often a handoff to Claude for analysis or writing. Perplexity establishes the facts. Claude does the thinking. That division of labor is the most powerful AI research workflow available.
